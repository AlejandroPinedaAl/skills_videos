# Scene Rhythm — Pacing Framework for Short-Form Video Ads

## Why Rhythm Matters More Than Copy
In long-form, you can write your way to conversion. In short-form (9-34s), the edit IS the message. If scenes are too long, viewers drop. If cuts are too fast, message doesn't land. This skill defines the exact timing for each narrative beat.

**Rule:** Every scene change resets attention. Use it strategically.

---

## Base Rhythm Model (9–15 seconds)

```
SCENE 1   0 – 2s   HOOK
├── Fastest scene — one single image, one single idea
├── Maximum 1 cut within this scene
├── If viewer leaves, they leave here — make it count
└── Target emotion: curiosity, recognition, or surprise

SCENE 2   2 – 4s   CONTEXT
├── Establish who this is for or what world we're in
├── Can be silent with text overlay
└── Target emotion: "this is about me"

SCENE 3   4 – 7s   MECHANISM / DEMO
├── Longest scene — show the product doing something
├── Can contain 2-3 cuts of the same action
└── Target emotion: understanding, desire

SCENE 4   7 – 10s  PROOF
├── Evidence that the mechanism works
├── Number, result, testimonial line, or before/after
└── Target emotion: belief, trust

SCENE 5   10 – 15s OFFER + CTA
├── Clean final scene — no new information
├── Visual: product + CTA button
└── Target emotion: urgency, action
```

---

## Extended Rhythm (21–34 seconds)

```
SCENE 1   0 – 2s    Hook (same as 15s model)
SCENE 2   2 – 5s    Context + problem setup
SCENE 3   5 – 10s   Story beat or deeper problem
SCENE 4   10 – 17s  Solution + demo (can split into 2 sub-scenes)
SCENE 5   17 – 24s  Proof + social validation
SCENE 6   24 – 30s  Offer framing
SCENE 7   30 – 34s  CTA
```

## Cut Timing by Platform

| Platform | Avg Cut Duration | Max Scene Length | Min Cuts Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok (9-15s) | 1.5–2s | 4s | 5 |
| TikTok (21-34s) | 2–3s | 6s | 8 |
| Meta Feed (15s) | 2–3s | 5s | 5 |
| Meta Feed (30s) | 3–4s | 7s | 7 |

| YouTube Skippable (15-30s) | 2–3s | 6s | 6 |

## Attention Decay Model
Human attention in short-form ads follows this curve:
- **0-2s:** Peak attention (hook window)
- **2-5s:** First drop (keep proving value)
- **5-8s:** Second window if hook earned it
- **8-12s:** Sustained only if emotional or logical investment was made
- **12-15s:** CTA window — earned by everything before

**Implication:** If you have a weak second scene, the viewer is already gone by the time you deliver your best content.

---

## Transition Types and When to Use

| Transition | Use When |
|---|---|
| Hard cut | Fast pacing, high energy, TikTok |
| Jump cut (same subject) | Building energy within one person talking |
| Match cut (visual rhyme) | Before/after reveals, elegant contrast |
| Text card insert | Adding emphasis, B2B, faceless format |
| Zoom in | Directing focus to a detail |
| B-roll cutaway | Supporting narration without breaking flow |
