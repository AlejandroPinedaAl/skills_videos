# PAS Short-Form — Problem → Agitate → Solution

## Framework Logic
PAS works because it mirrors the customer's mental state: they already HAVE the problem, they just need someone to name it precisely before they'll listen to the solution. The job of this framework is to make them feel seen in the first 3 seconds.

**Key insight for video:** The "Agitate" block must be visual or emotional — not logical. Don't explain why the problem is bad. Show the consequence or name the feeling.

---

## Structure (15-second version)

```
PROBLEM    0 – 3s
├── Name the exact situation, not the category
├── Use second-person: "tus", "estás", "tu negocio"
├── Be hyper-specific: platform + action + result
└── Example: "¿Estás publicando en Meta todos los días y no recibes ni un mensaje?"

AGITATE    3 – 8s
├── Escalate the consequence — what happens if this continues
├── Name the emotion: frustración, vergüenza, pérdida, miedo
├── Add a number or timeline to make it concrete
└── Example: "Cada día que pasa, estás regalándole ese presupuesto a la plataforma
             mientras tu competencia sí está convirtiendo."

SOLUTION   8 – 13s
├── Introduce the mechanism — not just the product, but HOW it fixes it
├── One sentence max. If it needs more, compress harder.
├── Visual: show the product working, the dashboard, the result
└── Example: "Con SaleADS, el sistema genera tus anuncios según tu negocio,
             tu audiencia y tu objetivo — en minutos."

CTA        13 – 15s
├── One action. One sentence.
├── Create micro-urgency if possible
└── Example: "Activa tu primera campaña gratis hoy."
```

---

## Structure (30-second version)

```
PROBLEM    0 – 5s     Extended setup with more context
AGITATE    5 – 15s    Two agitation beats: consequence + emotional cost
SOLUTION   15 – 25s   Mechanism + mini-demo or proof point
CTA        25 – 30s   Offer + action
```

## PAS by Business Type

### Physical Product
- **Problem:** The situation before having the product
- **Agitate:** The ongoing cost/frustration of NOT having it
- **Solution:** Product in use, showing the fix
- Example category: supplements, beauty, tools, food

### Service (Agency / Consulting)
- **Problem:** The specific operational failure (leads not converting, ads not working, team wasting time)
- **Agitate:** Money or time being wasted per day/week
- **Solution:** The method + proof of result
- Example category: marketing agencies, coaches, automation tools

### Local Business (Clinic, Restaurant, Gym)
- **Problem:** Customer's specific frustration (waited too long, couldn't find, paid too much)
- **Agitate:** The alternative they settled for
- **Solution:** What's different here + what to do now

### SaaS / App
- **Problem:** Manual process or broken workflow
- **Agitate:** Hours/week wasted or errors caused
- **Solution:** What the software does in one specific scenario

## Agitation Formulas (pick one per script)

| Type | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Financial | "Eso son $[X] al mes que se van sin retorno" | "Eso son $300 al mes que se van sin retorno." |
| Time | "Pierdes [X] horas a la semana en [task]" | "Pierdes 6 horas a la semana haciendo reportes a mano." |
| Competitor | "Mientras [you do X], tu competencia [does Y]" | "Mientras tú experimentas, tu competencia ya está escalando." |
| Emotional | "Y lo peor no es [X]. Lo peor es [emotional consequence]" | "Y lo peor no es gastar de más. Lo peor es no saber por qué." |
| Accumulation | "Día 1 [bad thing]. Día 30 [worse thing]. Día 90 [worst thing]." | |
